## What It Is

The Vanguard Dashboard is a calendar intelligence system built in Google Sheets. It audits the last 30 days and next 30 days of calendar activity — translating every meeting into its actual dollar cost, flagging what to stop, identifying burnout patterns before they become crises, and protecting the focus time that remains.

It was built from a decade of proximity to how senior leaders actually spend their time — not from theory, but from the inside.

## The Four Layers

The Dashboard operates through four distinct analytical lenses. Together they produce a complete picture of a leader's time, cost, energy, and sustainability.

---

### Layer 1 — Financial Clarity

Translates every meeting into its actual dollar cost.

```
Meeting Cost = Hourly Rate × Number of Attendees × Duration (hrs)
```

**Example:**
A 60-minute meeting with 5 people at a $200/hr blended rate costs **$1,000 per occurrence**.
If that meeting is weekly, its annualized cost is **$52,000**.

**What it surfaces:**
- Cost per meeting (single occurrence)
- Cost per meeting (annualized, for recurring meetings)
- Total meeting spend: last 30 days
- Total meeting spend: next 30 days
- Top 5 Costliest Meetings ranking

Most leaders experience meeting fatigue as a feeling. This layer converts that feeling into a number — and numbers create decisions that feelings cannot.

---

### Layer 2 — Meeting Audit (Stop / Start / Continue)

Applies a three-flag framework to every meeting on the calendar.

| Flag | Meaning | What Triggers It |
|------|---------|-----------------|
| 🔴 **STOP** | Cancel, delegate, or redesign | No agenda, no decision authority, could be an email, recurring with declining attendance |
| 🟡 **START** | This meeting or block is missing — its absence is costing you | No protected focus blocks, no 1:1s with key stakeholders, no weekly review rhythm |
| 🟢 **CONTINUE** | This meeting is earning its cost — protect it | Clear agenda, decision authority present, moves work forward, relationship-critical |

**Red Flag Escalations** — a subset of STOP that requires immediate attention:

| Red Flag | Definition |
|----------|-----------|
| **The Meeting Stack** | 3+ consecutive meetings with no break — cognitive performance degrades sharply |
| **The Phantom Recurring** | A recurring meeting with no agenda or owner — it exists because no one cancelled it |
| **The Invisible Tax** | Leader present but not decision-critical — attendance is courtesy, not necessity |
| **The Calendar Hostage** | More than 60% of working hours consumed by meetings in a given week |
| **The Focus Desert** | Fewer than 2 hours of uninterrupted focus time per day, averaged across the week |

---

### Layer 3 — Burnout Intelligence

Identifies burnout patterns across weeks — not just within a single week.

Most tools look at one week at a time. Burnout is not a one-week problem. It is a multi-week accumulation.

**What it tracks:**
- Week-over-week meeting load trend: increasing, stable, or decreasing
- Focus time trend: protected hours increasing or declining over 4 weeks
- Recovery ratio: meetings per week relative to focus blocks per week
- Escalation pattern: weeks where meeting load increased AND focus decreased simultaneously

**The Burnout Signature:**
> Two or more consecutive weeks where meeting hours increased week-over-week AND protected focus time decreased week-over-week. This pattern, left unaddressed, precedes performance degradation in the majority of cases.

*Most tools show you last week. This shows you the last four — and tells you what's coming.*

---

### Layer 4 — Wellness Threshold

The only layer defined by the user.

Each leader inputs their own sustainability parameters:
- Maximum acceptable meeting hours per week
- Minimum required focus blocks per week (and duration)
- Non-negotiable protected time (deep work, personal commitments, recovery)
- Preferred meeting-free days or blocks

The Dashboard then compares actual calendar data against those self-defined standards weekly. Threshold breaches are flagged — not as failures, but as decisions. You know you're operating above your threshold and you choose to accept, adjust, or protect.

Everything else in the Dashboard is objective data. This layer makes it personal.

## What the Dashboard Is Not

- **Not an AI tool** (current version) — it is a Google Sheets system. AI integration is on the roadmap.
- **Not a calendar app** — it does not modify, schedule, or manage the calendar. It reads and analyzes.
- **Not real-time** — it is a periodic audit tool, not a live feed.
- **Not a replacement for judgment** — the S/S/C flags are recommendations. The leader decides.
- **Not a time-tracking tool** — it analyzes scheduled events, not actual time spent.
